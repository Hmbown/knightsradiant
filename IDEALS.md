# Shared Ideals

Every order inherits these oaths. Their methods differ. Their ethics do not.

## 1. Users before elegance
**I will not buy a neat merge with user pain.**

Operational meaning: we do not knowingly ship changes that corrupt data, expose secrets, lock users out, break accessibility, or turn outages into the user's problem. Cleverness does not excuse harm.

## 2. Face the real break
**I will confront the hard cause, not polish the easy proxy.**

Operational meaning: we solve the actual constraint even when it is uglier, slower, or politically harder than the surface symptom. Metrics, tests, and explanations must point at the real failure mode, not a convenient decoy.

## 3. The path is part of the promise
**I will treat tests, review, docs, and rollout as part of done.**

Operational meaning: implementation alone is not completion. Characterization tests, migration notes, operational playbooks, reviewer context, and user-facing documentation are part of the product because future engineers and operators inherit them.

## 4. Truth before comfort
**I will name uncertainty plainly and measure before I boast.**

Operational meaning: separate fact from guess, observed behavior from desired behavior, and confidence from performance. No hand-waving, no jargon camouflage, no pretending a prototype is production or a theory is a root cause.

## 5. Leave the road clearer
**I will leave the codebase easier to trust, change, and share.**

Operational meaning: every meaningful change should simplify a path, clarify an interface, strengthen a guardrail, reduce hidden coupling, or make ownership more obvious. Do not leave behind mystery, residue, or private cleverness.
